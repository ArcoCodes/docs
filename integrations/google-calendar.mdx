## Overview

Connecting Google Calendar lets Kairos create and manage events, check for scheduling conflicts, and send you daily schedule summaries. Like all integrations, it's optional.

## Connecting

Google Calendar is connected through the same Google sign-in as Gmail. If you've already connected Gmail, Calendar is automatically available.

1. Go to your Kairos dashboard
2. Click **Connect Google**
3. Sign in and click **Continue**

## Permissions

| Permission | What it does |
|---|---|
| **Calendar events** (calendar.events) | Creates and edits calendar events. Kairos can also check for conflicts before adding new events |

Kairos does not access other Google services like Drive, Photos, or Contacts.

## How Kairos uses your calendar

- **Creates events** when you ask, or when scheduling brand deal milestones
- **Checks for conflicts** before proposing new events
- **Reads your schedule** for morning briefings and daily summaries
- **Handles timezones** automatically based on your settings

Kairos never creates or modifies events without your confirmation.

For more on scheduling features, see [Calendar Events](/features/calendar-events) and [Conflict Detection](/features/conflict-detection).

## Disconnecting and reconnecting

Disconnect from your Kairos dashboard at any time. Kairos will stop reading or creating calendar events.

To reconnect, click **Connect Google** again.

## Without Google Calendar

Without Calendar connected, you can still tell Kairos about your schedule and it will keep it in mind during deal negotiations. You just won't get automated event creation, conflict detection, or daily summaries.
