## Overview

Notion gives Kairos a place to store and organize your brand deal data: evaluation reports, negotiation logs, contract notes, deliverable tracking, and payment records. It's optional, but if you like having everything in one place, it's worth connecting.

## Connecting

1. Go to your Kairos dashboard
2. Click **Connect Notion**
3. Select the Notion workspace you want to use
4. Grant access to the pages Kairos needs

## What Kairos creates in Notion

### Brand deals database

The first time you evaluate a deal, Kairos sets up a brand deals database (or adapts to one you already have). Each deal gets its own page with:

- Brand evaluation report
- Fit assessment and pricing suggestions
- Negotiation log (updated as conversations progress)
- Scheduling milestones
- Contract review notes
- Deliverable URLs and delivery dates
- Payment records

### Smart setup

Kairos is designed to work with your existing Notion structure:

- If you already have a database, Kairos matches its columns by name and meaning
- Missing columns are added automatically
- Your existing columns are never deleted or renamed
- New deals get their own pages without disrupting your layout

## Disconnecting and reconnecting

Disconnect from your Kairos dashboard at any time. Your Notion data stays exactly as it is. Kairos simply stops reading or writing to it.

To reconnect, click **Connect Notion** again and re-authorize.

## Without Notion

Without Notion, Kairos still handles deal detection, evaluation, email drafting, and everything else. You just won't have the persistent deal tracking and organized records that Notion provides. All the information is still available in your Telegram conversation history.
