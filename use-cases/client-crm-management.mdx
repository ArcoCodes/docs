## The scene

You're a freelancer with a growing client list. Some have projects due next week, others are waiting on invoices, and a few haven't replied in days. You've been tracking everything in a spreadsheet, but it's getting unwieldy. You forget to follow up with one client and almost miss a deadline for another.

## What happens

You tell Kairos about your clients as they come in: "New client: Tanaka Corp, contact is tanaka@example.com, project deadline is April 15."

Kairos creates a client record in Notion with all the details. As the project progresses, you update it naturally: "Mark Tanaka Corp as in progress" or "Add a note: they requested an extension to April 22."

When deadlines approach, Kairos sends you a reminder. When you need an overview, just ask: "Show me all clients with deadlines this month."

No spreadsheets, no switching between apps. Everything lives in one place and stays up to date through conversation.

## Why this matters

Managing clients well is what keeps them coming back. But the admin side — tracking who needs what by when — eats into the time you could spend doing actual work. Kairos handles the bookkeeping so you can focus on delivering results and taking on more clients.
