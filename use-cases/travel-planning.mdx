## The scene

You've got a work trip coming up — meetings in two cities, a client dinner, and a day off squeezed in between. Flights, hotels, time zones, and ground transport all need to line up. You've got confirmation emails scattered across your inbox, and you're not sure if the hotel checkout conflicts with your afternoon meeting.

## What happens

Forward your booking confirmations to Kairos. It reads the flight times, hotel check-in/check-out, and meeting invites, then builds a day-by-day itinerary on your Google Calendar.

"Add a dinner reservation at 7 PM on Thursday" — done. "Move the Friday meeting to 3 PM" — Kairos checks for conflicts, updates the event, and adjusts your buffer time.

Need to share the plan? Ask Kairos to summarize your itinerary and it sends you a clean overview: flights, accommodation, meetings, and free time, all in one message.

If plans change mid-trip — a flight delay, a cancelled meeting — just tell Kairos and it reorganizes your schedule on the fly.

## Why this matters

Travel planning is a puzzle of overlapping details. Miss one piece and the whole day falls apart. Kairos keeps every piece in sync so you can focus on the trip itself — whether that's nailing your meetings or actually enjoying your time off.
