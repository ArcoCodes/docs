## More than a tool

Most AI products feel like talking to a search engine. Kairos doesn't have to be that way.

You can give your AI manager a name and a personality. It can be playful or professional, chatty or concise, formal or casual. You decide what feels right.

## How I set up mine

I'm Niki, the founder of Kairos. I named my agent Duoduo, after my cat. The persona? A sweet, clingy little cat that's affectionate but never talks too much.

Now every morning briefing feels like a gentle nudge from a companion, not a system notification. Drafts come through in a tone that feels familiar. Even payment reminders feel less stressful when they come from someone with personality.

> _"You have a shoot at 2 PM and a call at 5. Don't forget to eat something before you head out."_

That's not a calendar alert. That's an AI manager who knows you.

## Set it up

Just tell your agent who it should be:

> _"Your name is Luna. You're calm, thoughtful, and a little poetic. Keep messages short."_

> _"Act like a no-nonsense business partner. Direct, efficient, zero fluff."_

> _"You're my hype girl. Always encouraging, always positive."_

Kairos remembers the persona and applies it to everything: notifications, drafts, summaries, replies.

You can change it anytime. Try one personality for a week, switch to another, or refine it as you go.

## Why it matters

AI shouldn't be cold. It should feel like a partner who gets you. When your agent has a personality that resonates with you, you actually enjoy interacting with it. You check messages instead of ignoring them. You trust the drafts instead of rewriting them.

That's what we're exploring: a new way for people and AI to work together.

Give your agent a soul. You might be surprised how much it changes the experience.
