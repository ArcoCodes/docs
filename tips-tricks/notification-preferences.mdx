## The problem

Your inbox is full. Your calendar is packed. If your AI manager notified you about everything, it would just become another source of noise.

The whole point is less distraction, not more.

## What you can control

Kairos lets you decide exactly what's worth a notification and what isn't. You set the rules, and your agent follows them.

### Email notifications

By default, Kairos notifies you about emails that look important: brand deal inquiries, payment confirmations, and time-sensitive requests. But you can customize this however you want:

> _"Only notify me about brand deal emails."_

> _"Skip anything that looks like a newsletter or automated email."_

> _"If an email mentions payment or invoice, always notify me immediately."_

### Schedule reminders

Your morning briefing, evening preview, and event reminders are all optional. You choose what to include, when to send them, and whether you want them at all.

> _"Send me a morning summary at 8 AM, weekdays only."_

> _"Don't remind me about recurring meetings, I already know about those."_

> _"Add an evening message with tomorrow's first event so I know when to set my alarm."_

### Brand deal alerts

If you get a lot of inbound, you can filter what rises to the top:

> _"Don't bother me with deals that don't mention a budget."_

> _"Only flag deals from brands with over 100K followers."_

> _"If a brand is in the fitness space, always let me know."_

## Find your balance

Some creators want to know everything. Others only want to hear about the big stuff. There's no right answer.

Start with the defaults and adjust as you go. If something feels noisy, tell your agent to dial it back. If you're missing things you care about, add a rule.

> _"You're sending me too many email notifications. Only the important ones."_

That's all it takes. Kairos adapts.
