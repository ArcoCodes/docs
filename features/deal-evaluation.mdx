## Know what you're getting into

Not every brand deal is worth your time. Kairos evaluates opportunities by researching the brand, assessing how well it fits your content, and suggesting a fair price — so you can make informed decisions quickly.

## How evaluation works

When you tap **Evaluate** on a deal notification (or ask your AI manager to evaluate a brand), here's what happens:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Profile check">
    Kairos checks if your creator profile is up to date. If you haven't set one up yet, it'll guide you through the process before continuing.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Brand research">
    Your AI manager investigates the brand across the web — checking company legitimacy, product reputation, and creator collaboration history.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fit assessment">
    Using your creator profile and the brand research, Kairos determines how well this brand aligns with your content, audience, and values.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pricing suggestion">
    Based on your creator tier, platform benchmarks, and the deal terms, Kairos suggests a pricing range.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Report saved">
    The full evaluation is saved to your Notion deal page (if connected), and you get a summary with action buttons.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Two research depths

When evaluating a brand, you can choose the level of investigation:

### Standard mode

A quick check covering the essentials (8–12 web searches):

- **Identity & legitimacy** — company info, domain verification, online presence
- **Product reputation** — review scores on G2, Trustpilot, Reddit, and similar platforms
- **Creator track record** — past sponsorships, affiliate programs, payment history

### Deep mode

A thorough investigation for bigger deals or unfamiliar brands (32+ web searches):

Everything in Standard, plus:

- **Company scale** — funding rounds, team size, user base
- **Pricing analysis** — their product pricing, whether it fits your audience
- **Recent news** — product launches, partnerships, layoffs, controversies
- **Social presence** — their own social media activity and engagement
- **Fit signals** — target audience, brand positioning, past collaborator profiles

<Tip>
  Kairos remembers your preferred research depth. After a few evaluations, it'll default to the mode you tend to choose — no need to pick every time.
</Tip>

## Fit assessment

Each evaluation produces a fit rating:

| Rating | What it means |
|---|---|
| **High fit** | Strong audience alignment, positive collaboration history, reasonable compensation, no red flags |
| **Medium fit** | Partial alignment, some manageable risk factors, terms may be negotiable |
| **Low fit** | Audience mismatch, multiple red flags, or positioning conflicts |

## Pricing suggestions

Kairos suggests a pricing range (never a single number) based on:

- Your creator tier and platform benchmarks
- The brand's stated budget (if mentioned)
- Deal-specific factors like usage rights, exclusivity, and rush delivery
- Your past pricing decisions (learned over time from your preferences)

<Info>
  Kairos only uses deal-specific information (compensation, deliverables, timeline) from what the brand actually told you — it never guesses or fabricates deal terms.
</Info>

## After evaluation

Once the evaluation is complete, you'll see a summary with:

- Fit assessment (High / Medium / Low)
- Key findings from the brand research
- Suggested pricing range
- Any red flags detected

And two buttons:
- **Interested** — Kairos drafts a reply and moves the deal to negotiation
- **Not interested** — the deal is marked as declined in Notion
