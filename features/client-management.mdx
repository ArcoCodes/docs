## Stay on top of every client

When you're juggling multiple clients — whether you're a freelancer, consultant, accountant, or lawyer — things slip through the cracks. Missed deadlines, forgotten follow-ups, lost contact details. Kairos uses Notion as a lightweight CRM so you can track client info, case status, deadlines, and communication history. All through natural conversation with your AI manager.

## What gets tracked

| Field | Details |
|---|---|
| **Client info** | Name, contact email, phone, company |
| **Case / project status** | Active, pending, completed, on hold |
| **Deadlines** | Due dates for deliverables, filings, reviews |
| **Communication history** | Email threads, meeting notes, key decisions |
| **Payment status** | Invoiced, paid, overdue |

## How to use

### Adding clients

Just tell your AI manager in plain language:

> "Add new client: Tanaka Corp, contact is tanaka@example.com, project starts next Monday"

Kairos creates the client entry and fills in the details. No forms to fill out.

### Updating status

> "Mark Tanaka Corp's project as completed"

> "Update Sarah's invoice to paid"

Kairos finds the right client record and makes the change.

### Getting reminders

Kairos tracks deadlines across all your clients. When a deadline is approaching, you get a notification with:

- Client name
- What's due
- How many days until the deadline

You can also ask anytime:

> "Show me all clients with upcoming deadlines this week"

> "Which projects are still marked as active?"

### Reviewing your pipeline

> "Give me a summary of all active clients"

> "How many projects did I complete this month?"

Kairos pulls from your client database and gives you a clear overview.

## Notion integration

Kairos creates a client database in your Notion workspace. Columns auto-adapt based on the information you provide — if you start mentioning contract values, a contract value column appears. If you track industries, that gets added too.

Everything stays organized without you having to design a database schema upfront.

## Without Notion

Basic client tracking works through conversation alone — Kairos remembers what you tell it using its [memory system](/features/memory-system). But for a persistent, searchable database you can browse and filter yourself, connecting Notion is recommended.

<Info>
  Client data stays in your private sandbox. Kairos never shares client information across users.
</Info>
