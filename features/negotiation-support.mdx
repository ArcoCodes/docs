## Negotiate with confidence

Once you're interested in a deal, Kairos helps you navigate the conversation. From pricing strategy to email drafts, your AI manager provides stage-specific advice while you stay in control of every decision.

## What Kairos helps with

Kairos carries forward the pricing suggestions from your [deal evaluation](/features/deal-evaluation) and uses them as a starting point for negotiation.

### Content direction

- Suggests content ideas that align with both your style and the brand's requirements
- Highlights your best-performing formats as a starting point
- Flags potential conflicts between brand asks and your content approach

### Timeline and scheduling

- Checks your Google Calendar for availability (if connected)
- Suggests a realistic production schedule with milestones
- After you confirm, creates calendar events and syncs them to your Notion deal page

<Info>
  Kairos never creates calendar events without asking you first. It always presents a plan and waits for your confirmation.
</Info>

## Email drafting

Throughout negotiation, Kairos can draft reply emails that:

- Incorporate insights from your evaluation report
- Match your communication style (learned from your past edits)
- Reference specific deal terms from the brand's email
- Keep the conversation threaded correctly in Gmail

Every draft is presented for your review with three options:
- **Send** — send the email as-is
- **Edit** — make changes before sending
- **Cancel** — discard the draft

## Notion tracking

If you have Notion connected, Kairos updates your deal page throughout the negotiation:

- Appends to the negotiation log with each conversation summary
- Updates deal status as the negotiation progresses
- Records agreed terms (deliverables, timeline, compensation)

This gives you a complete history of every deal conversation in one place.

## Your preferences matter

Kairos learns from how you negotiate:

- If you consistently push back on exclusivity, it'll flag exclusivity clauses early
- If you prefer a direct tone in emails, it'll draft accordingly
- If you always counter with a specific rate structure, it'll factor that into suggestions

**The more you use it, the better the advice gets.**
