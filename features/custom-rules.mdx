## Make it work your way

Custom rules let you define exactly how Kairos should handle specific situations. While the [memory system](/features/memory-system) learns from your behavior automatically, custom rules give you direct control.

## How to set rules

Just tell your AI manager in plain language:

- _"Never send emails longer than 3 paragraphs"_
- _"Always include my media kit link when replying to brand inquiries"_
- _"If a brand offers less than $1,000, decline automatically and move on"_
- _"Don't create calendar events on weekends"_
- _"When evaluating a brand deal, always use Deep research"_

Kairos stores these as persistent rules and applies them going forward.

## Examples by category

### Email rules
- _"Keep email replies under 200 words"_
- _"Use a professional but warm tone in all emails"_

### Brand deal rules
- _"Decline any deal that requires exclusivity over 14 days"_
- _"My minimum rate for Instagram Reels is $1,500"_

### Notification rules
- _"Only notify me about brand deal emails and payment confirmations"_
- _"Don't notify me about emails that aren't related to brand deals"_

### Schedule rules
- _"Don't schedule anything before 10 AM"_
- _"Always add a 30-minute buffer between meetings"_

## Managing rules

You can view, update, or remove rules anytime:

- _"What rules do I have set?"_
- _"Update my minimum rate to $2,000"_
- _"Remove the rule about weekend events"_

## Rules vs. memory

| | Custom rules | Memory system |
|---|---|---|
| **How they're set** | You tell Kairos explicitly | Learned automatically from your behavior |
| **Priority** | Highest — always followed | Applied unless overridden by a rule |
| **Best for** | Hard boundaries and must-haves | General preferences and style |
| **Examples** | "Minimum rate is $2,000" | Kairos notices you usually counter at $2,000+ |
