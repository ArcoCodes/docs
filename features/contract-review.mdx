## Understand what you're signing

When a brand sends a contract, Kairos breaks it down into plain language. It highlights the terms that matter most to creators, flags potential risks, and suggests what you might want to negotiate.

<Info>
  Kairos provides analysis to help you understand contracts, not legal advice. For complex or high-value deals, consider consulting a lawyer.
</Info>

## How it works

Kairos detects contract emails automatically (or you can share a contract directly). It analyzes the document and presents a clear summary of the key terms.

## What Kairos analyzes

Kairos reviews the key terms that matter most to creators — payment terms, exclusivity, usage rights, content approval, IP ownership, and more. For each term, it explains what it means in plain language and highlights anything that could affect you.

## Risk highlights

Kairos flags terms that commonly cause problems for creators:

- Exclusivity longer than 30 days without proportional compensation
- Unlimited usage rights or perpetual licensing
- No kill fee provision
- Vague revision clauses ("until brand is satisfied")
- Payment terms beyond Net 60
- Broad IP ownership transfers

Each flag comes with a plain-language explanation of why it matters and a suggestion for what to negotiate.

## Notion integration

When you have Notion connected, Kairos:

- Updates your deal status to "Contract"
- Creates a Contract Review sub-page with the full analysis
- Uploads the contract attachment for your records

## Negotiation suggestions

For each flagged term, Kairos suggests specific alternatives you can propose. For example:

- _"Exclusivity is 90 days across all platforms. Consider requesting category-only exclusivity or reducing to 30 days."_
- _"Usage rights are perpetual. Consider limiting to 12 months or requiring additional compensation for extensions."_

You can ask Kairos to draft a reply email with your counter-proposals, and it'll handle the formatting and context.
